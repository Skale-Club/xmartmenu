Place this field after `"private": true` and before `"scripts"`. The resulting package.json top-level structure must be:

```json

```

Do NOT change any other field. Do NOT reorder dependencies. Do NOT add or remove any package. This is a single-field addition.

Why this works: Next.js reads the `browserslist` field from package.json at build time and passes it to the underlying Babel/SWC transpilation and the `@babel/preset-env` equivalent. With modern targets, polyfills for things like `Promise`, `fetch`, `Array.from`, `Object.assign` — already native in evergreen browsers — are skipped. Expected outcome: polyfills chunk shrinks from 109 KB (38 KB gz) to approximately 60–80 KB (20–28 KB gz).
